This is a sample academic abstract about the impact of AI on education. 
Artificial Intelligence in Education (AIEd) has evolved rapidly, offering personalized learning paths and automated grading systems. However, the integration of these tools often faces resistance due to ethical concerns regarding data privacy and the potential loss of critical thinking skills. This paper analyzes 50 institutions over two years, finding that students using AI-driven tutors showed a 15% increase in test scores, while those in traditional settings remained stagnant. The study concludes that a hybrid model is most effective.
